TearBag

The Emotional Vault

Live site ↗ Source ↗

Problem — Most journaling apps are notes apps with a mood emoji bolted on: no real privacy model, and nothing that responds back.

Build — A full MERN application with JWT auth and time-locked "capsule" entries that stay masked server-side until their unlock date. Layered a Groq-hosted Llama-3 companion on top that reads each entry's emotional tone and replies in character, plus a Gratitude Garden that turns journaling streaks into a growing tree.

Result — Live in production across Vercel, Render, and MongoDB Atlas, with a glassmorphism UI and Framer Motion micro-interactions that hold up on mobile.

● Offline-first

CareCall

Medical Call Audio Summarizer

Source ↗

Problem — Clinical staff lose real hours transcribing and summarizing patient calls, and most AI transcription tools require sending that audio to a third-party API — a non-starter with patient data.

Build — A fully offline FastAPI microservice: faster-whisper for multilingual transcription across six languages, a locally hosted Mistral 7B via Ollama for structured summaries, and RapidFuzz matching against a 225K-entry medicine dataset to flag prescriptions mentioned on the call. Async job queue with polling, results stored in SQLite.

Result — Zero API cost and zero patient data leaving the machine — built for teams that can't compromise on privacy.

PythonFastAPIfaster-whisper Ollama · Mistral 7BRapidFuzzSQLite
● Shipped

MediSense AI

Multi-Disease Risk Prediction Platform

Source ↗

Problem — Early risk signals for conditions like heart disease and diabetes sit unused in clinical datasets — most people never see a risk estimate until symptoms are already serious.

Build — Trained four separate classifiers — heart disease, diabetes, chronic kidney disease, and Parkinson's — on real clinical datasets (UCI Cleveland, PIMA Indian, UCI CKD, UCI Oxford voice) through a full pipeline: EDA, preprocessing, feature engineering, stratified train/test split, 5-fold cross-validation, and evaluation on accuracy, AUC, and F1. Served through a Flask REST API to a React 18 frontend with a dedicated page per condition and an offline fallback engine.

Result — Wrote up the risk-prediction pipeline as an IEEE-format research paper alongside the shipped product.

PythonScikit-LearnFlask ReactPandas
